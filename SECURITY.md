# Security Policy

Ennote is a high-security environment. We take security seriously and are committed to working with security researchers to responsibly verify and address any potential vulnerabilities in our infrastructure and open-source tools.


## Reporting a Vulnerability

**Please do NOT open a public GitHub issue for security vulnerabilities.**

We welcome responsible disclosure. Please report all security vulnerabilities through our official channels:

* **Direct Email:** [security@ennote.io](mailto:security@ennote.io)
* **Web Form:** [https://ennote.io/contact](https://ennote.io/contact)

If your report contains sensitive information, please encrypt your payload using our public key:
* **PGP Key:** [https://ennote.io/.well-known/pgp-key.txt](https://ennote.io/.well-known/pgp-key.txt)

Our DevSecOps team will acknowledge receipt and provide a timeline for mitigation.

## Safe Harbor & Rules of Engagement

We support safe harbor for security researchers who act in good faith. If you conduct vulnerability research according to our policy, we will consider this research to be authorized and will not recommend or pursue legal action against you.

**Rules of Engagement:**
* Do not access or modify data that does not belong to you.
* Use your own test accounts.
* Do not perform DDoS, automated scanning that disrupts services, or social engineering attacks.
